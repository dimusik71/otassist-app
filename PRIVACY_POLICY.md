## 1. Introduction

This Privacy Policy explains how OT/AH Assessment App ("we", "our", "the App") collects, uses, discloses, and protects your personal information in accordance with the Australian Privacy Act 1988 and the Australian Privacy Principles (APPs).

We are committed to protecting the privacy and security of health information for occupational therapists, allied health professionals, and their clients.

When we collect your personal information, we will inform you:

We use your personal information to:

### 3.2 Secondary Purposes

- Social media platforms
- Any other third parties not listed above

## 6. Security of Personal Information (APP 11)

### 6.1 Technical Security Measures

We protect your information with:
- **Encryption at Rest**: Mobile data encrypted using device secure enclave (iOS Keychain, Android Keystore)
- **Encryption in Transit**: HTTPS/TLS for all data transmission
- **Authentication**: Multi-factor authentication (2FA) for healthcare professionals
- **Session Management**: 15-minute idle timeout, automatic session expiry
- **Access Controls**: Role-based access, user isolation
- **Rate Limiting**: Protection against brute force and abuse
- **Audit Logging**: Comprehensive tracking of all data access

### 6.2 Database Security

⚠️ **Current Status**: Database encryption at rest is planned but not yet implemented. We are migrating to encrypted database storage (SQLCipher or PostgreSQL with TDE).

### 6.3 File Storage

⚠️ **Current Status**: Uploaded photos and videos are stored with authentication requirements but file-level encryption is planned for future implementation.

### 6.4 Security Breach Notification

In the event of a data breach involving personal information, we will:
- Notify affected individuals within 30 days
- Notify the Office of the Australian Information Commissioner (OAIC)
- Provide information about the breach and remediation steps

## 8. Data Retention and Destruction (APP 11.2)

### 8.1 Retention Periods

We retain personal information for:
- **Adult Clients**: 7 years from last assessment
- **Child Clients (under 18)**: Until age 25 or 7 years from last assessment, whichever is longer
- **Healthcare Professional Data**: Duration of account plus 7 years

These periods comply with health records legislation and professional indemnity requirements.

### 8.2 Secure Destruction

When retention periods expire, we securely delete information by:
- Overwriting database records
- Deleting encrypted files
- Removing all backups
- Maintaining audit trail of deletion

### 8.3 Archival

You can archive clients to indicate they are inactive. Archived clients:
- Are hidden from active client lists
- Are included in retention period calculations
- Can be permanently deleted after retention period expires
